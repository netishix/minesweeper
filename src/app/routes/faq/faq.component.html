Frecuently Asked Questions:

Minesweeper is a single-player puzzle video game. The objective of the game is to clear a rectangular board containing hidden "mines" or bombs without detonating any of them, with help from clues about the number of neighboring mines in each field.
The game originates from the 1960s, and it has been written for many computing platforms in use today. It has many variations and offshoots.

To win the game, you must uncover all non-mined cells, at which point, the timer is stopped.

You loose when you uncover a mined cell. You can place Flags <> or Question Marks <> by right clicking a Cell.
Flags are useful for flagging suspicious cells that might contain a mine. On the other hand, question marks are useful for flagging cells you are not sure about their underlying content.
